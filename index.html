Pokemon History

1980

First steps

Pokémon is short for "Pocket Monsters", the original Japanese name, The Franchise has its roots in a gaming magazine in the early 1980s in Japan- Game Freak, started by Satoshi Tajiri and Ken Sugimori. Jajiri was the writer, while Sugimori was the illustrator.

Game boy

26 February 1996 marks the debut of the Pokémon franchise in the form of both those games on the Game boy in Japan. At its heart, Pocket Monsters: Red and Green was a very basic Japanese role-playing game.

1996

1990

Expansion Era

The 1990s saw the global expansion of Pokémon, with the introduction of the trading card game, animated TV series, and various merchandise. Pokémon became a cultural phenomenon worldwide.

Advanced Gaming

The early 2000s brought advancements in gaming technology, leading to the release of Pokémon games on advanced handheld consoles like the Nintendo DS.

2005

2010

Modern Pokémon

In the modern era, Pokémon continues to evolve with new games, animated series, and collaborations, remaining a beloved franchise worldwide.

Mobile Revolution

The rise of mobile gaming in the 2010s saw the launch of Pokémon GO, a mobile game that brought Pokémon into the real world through augmented reality.

2016
